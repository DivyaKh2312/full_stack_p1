Welcome to Rajasthan

Rajasthan, the land of kings, is known for its vibrant culture, majestic palaces, and ancient forts. Every city in Rajasthan tells a unique tale of India's royal past.

  • Jaipur - The Pink City.

    • Jaipur is a UNESCO World Heritage City, celebrated for its historical landmarks, including the Hawa Mahal and Amer Fort. Its vibrant bazaars are a delight for shoppers and cultural enthusiasts.


    Jaipur is a popular tourist destination in India, forming a part of the west Golden triangle tourist circuit along with Delhi and Agra.[14] The city serves as a gateway to other tourist destinations in Rajasthan like Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, Bikaner, Udaipur, Kota, Mount Abu and has two World heritage sites of Amer Fort and Jantar Mantar. On 6 July 2019, the city was named to the World Heritage Cities list.[15] It is also known as Paris of India. Due to its beauty C.V. Raman called it "Island of Glory".



  • Jodhpur - The Blue City.

    • Jodhpur is home to the magnificent Mehrangarh Fort, which overlooks a sea of blue-painted houses. The city's architectural marvels and desert landscapes offer a serene and majestic escape.


    Jodhpur is a famous tourist spot with a palace, fort, and temples, set in the stark landscape of the Thar Desert. It is also known as the 'Blue City' due to the dominant color scheme of its buildings in the old town.[1] The old city circles the Mehrangarh Fort and is bounded by a wall with several gates.[15] Jodhpur lies near the geographic centre of the Rajasthan state, which makes it a convenient base for travel in a region much frequented by tourists.
